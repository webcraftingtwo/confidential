01
Executive Summary

The Valterra SLAM Re-Entry App is a purpose-built digital safety platform designed to replace the current paper-based SLAM (Stop, Look, Assess, Manage) re-entry procedure used by underground mining crews at Unki Mine following primary blasts. The application digitizes Operational Procedure UNK-MIN-MIN-PRO-0002 Version 6.0 in its entirety, guiding Overseer Miners and their crews through all five procedural phases with real-time guided checklists, enforced critical step locking, gas reading capture, hazard identification, a live countdown timer, zone-based check-in, emergency SOS alerting, near-miss reporting, and mandatory supervisor sign-off before any record is submitted.

This document presents the full concept, feature set, safety technology rationale, compliance alignment, technology stack, and phased development roadmap. A fully functional interactive prototype has been developed and is available for demonstration.

Core purpose: Every re-entry submission is timestamped, attributed to a named officer, and stored permanently — fully aligned with Valterra's Record Control policy and applicable legal requirements under the Mine (Management and Safety) Regulations S.I. 109 of 1990 and Explosives Regulations S.I. 72 of 1989. Zero Harm is the foundational design principle of every feature in this application.
02
The Problem with Paper

The current hard-copy SLAM procedure introduces operational and compliance risks that grow more significant as Valterra's underground operations scale. These are not theoretical risks — they are documented failure modes of paper-based safety systems across the mining industry globally.

Current State — Paper Forms
Forms frequently completed retrospectively, not in real time underground
No enforcement mechanism to ensure critical steps are completed in sequence
Gas readings recorded manually with no threshold alerts or validation
Paper records lost, damaged, or illegible in underground conditions
No centralized audit trail accessible to SHE management or supervisors
30-minute initial re-entry time limit cannot be actively monitored
Supervisor sign-off unverifiable and not independently timestamped
Near-miss incidents chronically underreported due to friction in reporting
No mechanism for personnel location tracking or emergency response
Zero data aggregation — patterns and recurring hazards are invisible
Future State — Digital App
Procedure completed live — each step actively confirmed before progression
Sequential phase locking — critical steps physically impossible to skip
Gas readings captured digitally with legal limit references displayed per field
All submissions stored securely in cloud with full redundancy
SHE management dashboard provides real-time visibility of all records
Built-in 30-minute countdown timer with color-coded urgency escalation
Digital supervisor sign-off with automatic timestamp and identity attribution
One-tap near-miss reporting with structured incident type and severity capture
Zone-based check-in feeds directly into SOS emergency alert system
Aggregated data enables SHE analysis of trends, hazard patterns, and compliance gaps

How It Works — The Five Procedure Phases

The application mirrors the exact structure of Procedure UNK-MIN-MIN-PRO-0002 Version 6.0. Each phase must be completed in full before the next phase is unlocked. Critical steps — those with direct safety or legal implications — are flagged visually and enforced with a hard block. The miner cannot advance until every critical step is confirmed.

Phase Name Key Actions Critical Gates
9.1 Re-Entry Preparation Blast token check, watch synchronization, SCADA fan confirmation, 2-hour lapse verification, ventilation flap inspection, airflow check to waiting place 2-hr lapse confirmed · SCADA confirmed · Re-entry time on blasting board
9.2 Waiting Place Management Safety Talk with structured topic recording, Toolbox Talk sign-in, Team Talk, Attendance Register, PPE inspection, fitness for duty, hazard identification, duty assignment Toolbox Talk signed · PPE inspected · All personnel fit for duty · Safety Talk recorded
9.3 Initial Re-Entry NSS Box locked to NO BLAST, barricade procedure, joint ground assessment, PCU disconnect, airflow verification, continuous gas testing with MGDI readings captured, barricade installation NSS locked · PCU disconnected · Gas tests logged · 30-min timer enforced
9.4 Watering Down & Barring Down Gas test at blasted end, water blast operation with minimum 15-minute duration recorded, barring down per UNK-MIN-MIN-PRO-0003, ground condition notes No work under unsupported ground — hard enforced · Gas test mandatory
9.5 Searching & Handling Blown-Out Explosives Search for misfires by Blasting Licence holder, collection into Chola bag, disposal into section explosives box, misfire escalation form if applicable Blasting Licence holder only · Misfire escalation to PRO-0067 integrated
Critical Step Locking: Any step marked Critical cannot be bypassed. If a miner attempts to advance without completing all critical steps for the current phase, the app hard blocks progression, displays a red error banner listing every incomplete critical step by name, and visually highlights each unchecked item on screen. This is non-negotiable by design.

Current Features — Prototype v4.0

The following features are fully implemented and demonstrable in the current prototype. Features marked NEW were added following deep research into underground mining safety technology standards and the Zero Harm principle.

🔒
Critical Step Phase Locking
Hard enforcement of all critical procedure steps. Cannot advance without completion. Red error banner and visual highlighting of every unchecked critical item.
⏱
30-Minute Countdown Timer
Live timer activates at Phase 3. Color transitions from teal to amber to red as time elapses. Audio alert and on-screen warning if 30-minute limit is exceeded.
🧪
Gas Reading Capture
Digital entry for CO, NO2, O2, and H2S with legal threshold limits displayed per field. All readings stored against the submission record permanently.
🗣
Safety Talk Record
Structured Safety Talk documentation with 12 pre-set topic chips covering all procedure-relevant hazards, plus a free-text notes field for shift-specific details.
⚠️
Hazard Identification
Toggle-based hazard selection across 8 categories including fumes, bad hangings, dust, blown explosives, ventilation issues, ground instability, misfires, and FOG light issues.
✍️
Digital Supervisor Sign-Off
Mandatory final step. Supervisor name entered and digital confirmation tapped. Automatic timestamp attributed to both the submitting officer and the supervisor independently.
💾
localStorage Draft Saving
Returning users are recognized and auto-populated. Draft progress saves automatically at every step. If the app closes mid-procedure, it restores exactly where the miner left off.
🆘
SOS Emergency Alert NEW
Permanent SOS button visible on all phases. Two-step activation prevents accidental triggers. Alert includes officer name, badge ID, current zone, section, and timestamp. Fires to surface control immediately.
⚡
Near-Miss Reporting NEW
One-tap access from any phase. Structured form captures incident type, severity rating (Low / Medium / High), zone, description, and immediate corrective action. All reports appear in the supervisor sign-off summary.
📍
Zone Check-In System NEW
Persistent zone selector organized by section — North, South, Development. Zone data feeds automatically into SOS alerts and near-miss reports. Foundation for future RTLS hardware integration.
🔐
Role-Based Login
Identity captured at login including name, badge ID, role, section, and blasting licence number. Attributed to every record, sign-off, and SOS alert generated during the session.
📊
Submission Record Summary
Completion screen displays full record summary including officer, section, zone, near-miss count, reference number, and timestamp. Record locked and submitted on supervisor sign-off.

Safety Technology Research — Feature Roadmap

The following features have been identified through deep research into underground mining safety technology standards, global best practice, and the specific operational context of Unki Mine. They are organized into three priority tiers and form the development roadmap beyond the current prototype.

Tier 1 — Life Critical · Build Next
🆘
SOS Panic Button with Surface Escalation — Backend Integration
The SOS button is implemented in the prototype UI. The next step is connecting it to a real backend alert system — notifying the surface control room via push notification, SMS, or dashboard alert the moment a miner activates it. Alert payload includes officer identity, zone, section, timestamp, and current phase of procedure. A missed check-in escalation system runs alongside it: if a miner fails to check in every 30 minutes, an automatic alert is dispatched to the supervisor without the miner needing to act.
⚡
Near-Miss Backend Storage and SHE Dashboard Integration
Near-miss reports currently captured in-session need to be stored permanently in the database and surfaced on the SHE management dashboard. This transforms near-miss data from a per-shift note into a mine-wide safety intelligence asset. Research indicates mines using digital near-miss systems reduce compliance violations by up to 40% compared to paper-based workflows. Every report stored enables pattern analysis — which zones, which shift times, which hazard types recur most frequently.
Tier 2 — High Value · Phase 3–4
📸
Photo Evidence Capture with Automatic Timestamping
Camera integration allowing miners to attach photographic evidence to specific checklist steps — a photo of a bad hanging, a misfire location, a FOG light condition, or a gas reading display. Every photo is automatically stamped with the officer name, time, date, section, and zone. This creates a visual audit record that is legally defensible and practically invaluable during incident investigations. Research confirms photo-augmented digital safety reports reduce investigation time significantly compared to verbal descriptions on paper forms.
🎙
Voice-to-Text Note Entry
Underground with gloves on, dust on a screen, in poor lighting — typing is genuinely difficult and slows the procedure down. Voice entry for hazard descriptions, near-miss reports, and ground condition observations reduces friction and increases the quality and completeness of data captured. Modern voice-to-text accuracy exceeds 95% even in noisy environments. This feature directly addresses one of the most common failure points of digital safety tools underground: user compliance.
📍
Location Tracking — Zone-Based to Full RTLS
Standard GPS does not function underground due to the absence of satellite visibility. The zone check-in system currently implemented is the correct near-term approach — no hardware required, zero cost, immediate value in SOS response accuracy. The long-term roadmap involves BLE beacon infrastructure for zone-level presence detection and UWB (Ultra-Wideband) sensors for centimetre-level precision in critical areas such as blast zones, equipment bays, and re-entry corridors. This upgrade path is designed into the app architecture from day one — when Valterra invests in the hardware, the software is ready.
🔄
Digital Shift Handover
When one shift ends and another begins underground, critical information currently passes verbally or on paper — hazards found, unresolved issues, fresh barricades, FOG light conditions. A structured digital handover built into the app means the incoming Overseer Miner starts every shift with a complete, verified picture of the current state of their section. This single feature eliminates one of the most dangerous moments in underground mining: the knowledge gap between shifts.
Tier 3 — Competitive Edge · Long-Term Vision
⌚
Wearable Integration — Smart Helmets and Biometric Vests
The global mining industry is moving toward wearable safety technology — smart helmets and vests that monitor heart rate, breathing, fatigue levels, and gas exposure in real time. The SLAM app is designed from the ground up to eventually receive this data as input, automatically populating gas readings from MGDI wearable instruments and flagging fatigue-related risk before a miner enters a blast zone. This is a 2 to 3 year horizon feature, but the app architecture accommodates it today.
🤖
Predictive Hazard Intelligence — AI Layer
Once 6 to 12 months of submission data is accumulated, machine learning algorithms can identify where and when specific hazard types are most likely to occur — based on section, shift time, blast size, weather conditions, and crew history. Predictive alerts can be surfaced to supervisors before a shift begins: "North Panel N2 has recorded gas exceedances in 3 of the last 5 re-entries on this shift pattern — recommend additional MGDI checks." This is the difference between reactive safety management and genuinely predictive zero harm.

Proposed Technology Stack

The application is built on modern, industry-proven technologies chosen for reliability, security, cost-effectiveness, offline capability, and maintainability. The stack is designed to scale from a single-section pilot to a full mine deployment without architectural changes.

Frontend
React Native (Expo)
Single codebase delivers native iOS and Android apps plus a web browser version — eliminating the cost and overhead of maintaining separate codebases. Expo managed workflow enables rapid iteration and over-the-air updates without app store submissions.
Database
Supabase (PostgreSQL)
Open-source backend platform providing a structured relational database, real-time data sync, and file storage for photo evidence. Free tier is generous for pilot phase. Fully self-hostable if Valterra requires data to remain on-premise.
Auth
Supabase Auth
Role-based authentication ensuring only verified Valterra employees can access the system. Supports email, employee ID, or future integration with Valterra's existing SSO or Active Directory.
Offline
Local-First Sync
Forms saved locally on the device when underground without network connectivity. Automatic background sync to the database the moment signal is restored. Critical for underground environments where connectivity is unreliable or absent entirely.
Alerts
Push + SMS Gateway
SOS alerts and missed check-in escalations delivered via push notification to supervisor devices and SMS to the surface control room. Twilio or Africa's Talking SMS gateway for reliable delivery in the Zimbabwe network environment.
Dashboard
React Web App
Browser-based SHE management dashboard. No installation required for supervisors and managers — accessible from any device on the mine network. Record search, filter by date and section, export to PDF, compliance reporting, and near-miss trend analysis.

Value to Valterra — Zero Harm First

Beyond operational efficiency, the SLAM Re-Entry App delivers measurable value across three dimensions that are critical to Valterra's mission and its obligations to every person who goes underground at Unki Mine.

🛡
Safety
Critical step locking eliminates skipped steps under time pressure. Gas threshold references give crews real-time situational awareness. SOS and zone check-in ensure no miner is unreachable in an emergency. Near-miss data prevents the next incident before it happens.
⚖️
Compliance
Every submission creates an immutable, timestamped digital record. In any safety incident, investigation, or regulatory audit, Valterra has a complete, searchable history of every re-entry — who performed it, what was found, what was done, and who signed off.
📈
Intelligence
Aggregated data enables SHE management to identify patterns invisible to paper systems — recurring hazard types, sections with gas exceedances, shifts with incomplete compliance. This insight is the foundation of predictive safety management.
Zero Harm is not aspirational — it is the design constraint. Every feature decision in this application was evaluated against the question: does this make it harder for a miner to be harmed underground? Critical step locking, the SOS button, near-miss reporting, zone check-in, and the 30-minute timer all exist for one reason — to close the gaps where the current paper system allows risk to go undetected and unrecorded.
